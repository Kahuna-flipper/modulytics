The Modulytics website provides interactive visualizations of “iModulons” (independently modulated gene sets) that describe sets of coregulated genes in an organism’s transcriptional regulatory network (TRN). The iModulons were determined using independent component analysis (ICA), a signal deconvolution unsupervised learning algorithm, which identified unique signals from transcriptomics data.

Currently, Modulytics solely focuses on the iModulons from the pioneering paper on Escherichia coli that uses data from PRECISE (a Precision RNA-seq Expression Compendium for Independent Signal Exploration). In the future, Modulytics will expand its scope to other prokaryotes – stay tuned!

iModulon Dashboard Overview

For a description of each of the figures shown on the iModulon dashboards hover over the various components of the example “MalT” iModulon below:

Name and Description

Here, the iModulon name is given in large font. Below, several descriptors are given, including the biological function and category of iModulon. If the iModulon is found to map to a known regulator, then the "Regulated by" descriptor will be followed by the regulator name.

Gene Table

The gene table lists the genes that are included in the iModulon. Working from left to right, the first column gives the gene number (b-numbers for E. coli), the second column ("Mi") gives the ICA-determined weight of the gene in the iModulon, the third column ("Name") gives the gene names, and the next column will be a checklist of whether the iModulon gene is in the regulon for regulatory iModulons only. After the first 3-4 columns, the "Product" column gives the expected product of the gene, the "Operon" column gives any other genes in the same operon as iModulon gene (where applicable), and the "TF" column (not shown here - need to scroll right) gives the known transcription factors associated with the iModulon gene. Clicking on a row will take you to a page with more information on the iModulon gene, such as EcoCyc for E. coli.

Genes

Gene Weight Histogram

For the statistically-determined signal, each gene has a "weight" that represents its importance towards the signal. Genes with weights that occur above a determined threshold are considered to be a part of the iModulon. The histogram shows the distribution of gene weights for the particular signal that is associated with the iModulon. Genes that fall to the left or right of the region between vertical bars are in the iModulon. On the dashboard page, you can hover over the iModulon gene weight bars to see the associated genes. If the iModulon has a regulator enrichment, the genes in the regulon (per RegulonDB for E. coli) will be shown in blue.

Gene Weight Scatter Plot

Similar to the gene histogram, the gene scatter plot shows the weight of each gene towards the signal. In this figure, the gene weights are plotted against the corresponding mean gene expression at the control conditions. The iModulon genes are those that fall above or below the horizontal threshold line(s). On the dashboard page, the gene name and values can be displayed by hovering over each point.

Activity

Activity Bar Graph

The activity bar graph shows the relative activity level of the iModulon across all the experimental samples included in the dataset. The breakdown of the different studies contributing to the activity profile are included below the x-axis, where the vertical lines indicate the split of different studies that the various samples fall into. The black points represent each biological replicate for the sample, and the blue bars represent the averaged activity from the biological replicates. Hovering over the data shows the sample name, the activity value, and the associated carbon source of the sample (if known). Clicking on the blue data bars will take you to the paper describing the associated study.

The hamburger menu in the top right of the figure includes additional options, such as the ability to view the figure in full screen or download the figure as either a pdf or png file. The menu also includes csv file downloads for the metadata that describes the various samples, and the underlying activity data for the iModulon.

Regulation

iModulon and Regulon Venn Diagram

If the iModulon is mapped to a regulator, the venn diagram appears to show the overlap between the genes in the iModulon and the regulon (per RegulonDB for E. coli). On the dashboard page, you can hover over each part of the venn diagram to see the number of genes and gene names associated with each group in the diagram.

Correlation to Regulator

In addition to the venn diagram, the correlation scatter plot appears if the iModulon can be mapped to a regulator. The plot shows the relationship between the iModulon activity level and the expression of the regulator gene across the various conditions. If the iModulon is correctly attributed to the regulator, a linear relationship is expected between activity and expression.
